How to read the collection

The point of this page is not to turn file counts into a fetish. It is to show how the public site relates to the private working archive. The public pages are the readable layer: longform essays, compact framing texts, and a shared references library. The private repo is the research layer: local copies of sources, tracked full-text gaps, evolving working documents, and source audits.

That distinction matters because the site is meant to function as a publication surface, not as a raw dump of PDFs. The archive tells you what kinds of evidence are actually being maintained, where the project is strongest, and which strands are still provisional. In that sense, this page is less a catalog than a map of how argument, evidence, and ongoing collection work fit together.

Canonical working docs

The working repository is organized around three main writing tracks plus a supporting audit layer. The broad synthesis lives in the overview article, the applied design translation lives in the pain proposal, and the installation-facing translation lives in the ETH application set.

  1. overview-article/ currently holds three files and anchors the broad public-facing argument. The public counterpart is the overview essay.
  2. proteus-vr-pain-treatment/ currently holds one canonical proposal file. The public counterpart is the pain page.
  3. eth-phd-application/ currently holds 30 files spanning notes, supporting documents, ready-to-upload files, and main application materials. This material remains largely private and application-specific.
  4. source-audits/ currently holds six files: source audits and claim matrices for the overview article, pain proposal, and ETH application.

Maintained source archive

The source archive is grouped into five topical folders. The current balance of the archive shows where the project is strongest today: XR embodiment, avatar design, pain modulation, and a media-theory line that now includes explicit animation-craft support for physically grounded stylization.

  1. vr-embodiment-and-avatar-design/ currently holds 30 files and is the largest topic cluster. It now includes an expanded John Desnoyers-Stewart line from 2019 to 2025, covering point-cloud mirrors, Body RemiXer, Star-Stuff, ETC, and later performance or atmospheric practice. It also now includes Bernal and Maes's Emotional Beasts, which strengthens the archive around expressive nonhuman avatar form, second skins, and physiology-linked transformation (Transcending Projection; Virtual Mirror Stage; Body RemiXer; Star-Stuff; ETC; Emotional Beasts).
  2. pain-body-representation-and-virtual-embodiment/ currently holds 14 files and contains the most directly relevant chronic-pain sources (Ho et al.; Lewis et al.; Martini et al.; Solca et al.; Bullington).
  3. avatar-perception-and-simulation-foundations/ currently holds 12 files and supplies the coherence, simulation, and behavior layer (Wagemans et al.; Johansson; Reeves; Reynolds; Yee and Bailenson).
  4. media-theory-and-plasmatic-aesthetics/ currently holds 12 files and now anchors not only the historical genealogy of plasmatic, spectral, and animated form, but also a more explicit animation-craft rule for believable transformation through the two Elemental Magic volumes (Eisenstein; Geil; Whissel; Wilson; Bukatman; Owens; Gilland 2009; Gilland 2012).
  5. pain-and-psychedelic-interfaces/ currently holds four files and frames the bridge from boundary-softened embodiment to connectedness and altered-state design (Glowacki et al.; Glowacki).

Audit status and current gaps

The private repo is no longer just a pile of files. It already includes a lightweight audit layer: source audits, claim matrices, and a deliberately short missing-fulltext tracker. That audit state is one of the most important parts of the collection because it shows where the archive is already strong and where it is still provisional.

  1. Six audit documents currently exist: a source audit and claim matrix each for the overview article, pain proposal, and ETH application.
  2. Four high-value missing full texts remain on the queue: Ho 2022, Lewis 2021, Solca 2018, and Lecuyer 2009 (Ho et al.; Lewis et al.; Solca et al.; Lecuyer).
  3. Two notable files were resolved in the latest pass: ETC and Body RemiXer now have local full-text copies, which strengthens the archive around abstract social bodies and aura-touch interaction (ETC; Body RemiXer).

Publicly, that audit state is just as important as the sources themselves. It shows that the project is not treating references as decorative backing for prewritten claims. The archive is being used as an active constraint on what can be stated confidently, what remains a hypothesis, and where future acquisition work still matters.

What is not public here

Not everything in the private repo should be mirrored directly.

  1. tmp/ is intentionally excluded from the public site because it contains working extractions, previews, user-provided text copies, and one-off processing output.
  2. background-references/ is currently just a staging folder with its README; nothing substantive has been promoted there yet.
  3. Media/ is initialized but still mostly structural: a README, two subfolder READMEs, and a license-audit note. No substantial public media archive exists yet.
  4. Some private working documents, especially in the ETH application set, remain too application-specific to mirror directly. The public site exposes their role in the collection rather than reproducing them.

The immediate public direction is not just to add more pages, but to keep turning the private archive into readable public argument. The value of the site is that it makes the research legible without dissolving the distinction between working archive and published prose.
